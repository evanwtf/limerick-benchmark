There once was a man from Nantucket
Who kept all his cash in a bucket.
    But his daughter, named Nan,
    Ran away with a man
And as for the bucket, Nantucket.

A wonderful bird is the pelican,
His beak holds more than his belican.
    He can take in his beak
    Food enough for a week,
But I'll be darned if I know how the helican.

There once was a girl from Peru
Who dreamed she was eating her shoe.
    She woke with a fright
    In the middle of the night
And found it was perfectly true.

A flea and a fly in a flue
Were caught, so what could they do?
    Said the fly, "Let us flee!"
    "Let us fly!" said the flea.
So they flew through a flaw in the flue.

There was an old man with a beard
Who said, "It is just as I feared!
    Two owls and a hen,
    Four larks and a wren,
Have all built their nests in my beard!"

A canner, exceedingly canny,
One morning remarked to his granny,
    "A canner can can
    Anything that he can,
But a canner can't can a can, can he?"

There once was a coder named Dwight
Whose tests would all pass overnight.
    But the bugs, as you'd guess,
    Made an absolute mess
The moment he pushed into flight.

There once was a fellow named Hall
Who fell in the spring in the fall.
    'Twould have been a sad thing
    Had he died in the spring,
But he didn't — he died in the fall.

A tutor who tooted a flute
Tried to tutor two tooters to toot.
    Said the two to the tutor,
    "Is it harder to toot, or
To tutor two tooters to toot?"

There once was a lady from Crete
Who was exceedingly neat.
    When she got out of bed
    She stood on her head
To make sure she wouldn't crease the sheet.

A painter who lived in Great Britain
Interrupted two girls with their knittin'.
    He said with a sigh,
    "That park bench — well I
Just painted it, right where you're sittin'."

There was a young lady of Lynn
Who was so uncommonly thin
    That when she essayed
    To drink lemonade,
She slipped through the straw and fell in.

There was an old person of Ware
Who rode on the back of a bear.
    When they asked, "Does it trot?"
    He said, "Certainly not!
It's a Moppsikon Floppsikon bear!"

That a chemist had poisoned his brain.
    The cause of his sorrow

Who dreamed he was eating his bed.
    He awoke in the night
    With a terrible fright
And found it was perfectly — dread.

There was a young fellow of Perth
Who was born on the day of his birth.
    He was married, they say,
    On his wife's wedding day,
And he died when he quitted the earth.

A daring young lady of Guam
Observed, "The Pacific's so calm
    I'll swim out for a lark."
    She met a large shark.
Let us now sing the ninetieth psalm.

There was a young man of Japan
Whose limericks never would scan.
    When they said it was so,
    He replied, "Yes, I know,
But I always try to fit as many words into the last line as I possibly can."

There once was a server on port
Eight-one-eight-one — short and sweet sort.
    It served up a page
    That refreshed on a stage
Every five seconds, as one might retort.

A programmer working in Flask
Took on an ambitious task.
    "Random limericks, served hot,
    Updating a lot —
Port eighty-one-eighty-one, if you ask."
